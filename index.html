    After the Quota Reform Movement in Bangladesh, Nobel laureate Dr. Muhammad Yunus, serving as the Chief Advisor of the interim government, addressed the nation with a significant speech. In this address, he emphasized the need for constitutional reforms and changes in various state sectors to reflect the demands of the July uprising. Dr. Yunus stated, "The July uprising has sent a clear message. To honor this, we need reforms in our constitution and governance structures." He presented plans for ensuring political stability, good governance, and long-term development for the country.
